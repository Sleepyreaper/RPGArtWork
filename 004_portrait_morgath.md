# Brief 004 — Portrait: Morgath the Harbinger

**Asset:** `portrait_morgath.png`
**Canvas:** 192 × 48 px (4 frames in a row, each 48 × 48)
**Frame order:** Neutral, Sad, Cold, Furious
**Format:** PNG with alpha + `.procreate` source

> Note: Different emotion order than the party portraits. Morgath is
> never *happy* in the player's view. He's tired, mournful, distant, or
> dangerous.

## Character

Morgath, age ambiguous (looks 50s but is centuries old). The
antagonist. He is NOT a snarling villain — he is a man who once tried to
save the world, decided it couldn't be saved, and is now acting on that
conclusion with terrifying calm.

- **Hair:** ash-grey, long, swept back from a high forehead. Almost
  white but with darker streaks remaining. Slightly unkempt.
- **Eyes:** deep set, dark amber-brown. The shadows under his eyes are
  permanent — he hasn't slept properly in decades.
- **Skin:** pale and weathered, a few age lines around the eyes and
  mouth.
- **Clothes (visible at portrait crop):** high-collared black robe with
  crimson interior lining (visible at the collar opening). A small
  iron-grey pendant — broken silver crescent on a black cord.
- **Posture:** absolutely still. Head straight, chin slightly raised.
  The kind of stillness that comes from someone who has stopped trying
  to be approachable.

## Emotion breakdown

| Frame | Emotion | What sells it |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Neutral (Tired)** | Eyes half-lidded, mouth a slight downturn. He looks like he's been awake too long, for too many years. NOT angry — just done. |
| 2 | **Sad** | Eyes fully visible now, looking off-frame. Brows lifted slightly in the middle (the "I've seen too much" expression). Mouth softens. This is the most human he ever looks. |
| 3 | **Cold** | Eyes sharp and forward. No anger — just judgment. The expression a doctor makes telling you the diagnosis. THIS is his most common combat-state face. |
| 4 | **Furious** | RARE. The mask cracks. Eyes wide and burning, mouth pulled back showing teeth. This is for the moment late in the story when his composure breaks. Make it scary precisely because it's UNLIKE the other three. |

## Notes

- Morgath's whole visual is "he looks like he could be your wise old
  mentor, not your villain". Resist the temptation to make him visually
  evil. The horror is that he isn't.
- The crimson lining is the only warm color in his palette — and it
  echoes the Wicked Moon. That symbolism matters; keep that color
  saturated.
- He should look like someone who was once handsome.
- The "Furious" frame is the one frame where the camera-stillness
  breaks. You can shift his shoulders forward, hair fall slightly out
  of place — make it visually clear the mask has come off.
